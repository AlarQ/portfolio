# Signs of AI Writing

Based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" guide. Use this to check if a text reads as AI-generated.

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing

## 1. Inflated Symbolism and Meaning

Words and phrases that overstate importance:

- "stands as a testament"
- "serves as a testament"
- "plays a vital / significant role"
- "underscores its importance"
- "continues to captivate"
- "leaves a lasting impact"
- "watershed moment"
- "key turning point"
- "deeply rooted"
- "profound heritage"
- "steadfast dedication"
- "solidifies"
- "battle-tested"
- "intricate"
- "crucial"
- "massive"

## 2. Promotional Language

Reads like advertising copy:

- "rich cultural heritage"
- "rich history"
- "breathtaking"
- "must-visit" / "must-see"
- "stunning natural beauty"
- "enduring legacy" / "lasting legacy"
- "rich cultural tapestry"

## 3. Editorializing

The text tells the reader what to think:

- "it's important to note / remember / consider"
- "it is worth"
- "no discussion would be complete without"
- "in this article"
- "what genuinely excites me"

## 4. Overused Conjunctive and Transition Phrases

Excessive connectors that pad the text:

- "on the other hand"
- "moreover"
- "in addition"
- "furthermore"
- "however"
- "in contrast"
- "in summary"
- "overall"
- "not only... but also..."

## 5. Negative Parallelism

The "not X, it's Y" structure, especially when repeated:

- "It is not just about X, it's about Y"
- "Not X specifically, but Y"
- "The hardest part is not X - it is Y"
- "This is not a nice-to-have - it is..."

One instance is fine. Two or more in the same text is a strong AI signal.

## 6. Superficial Analysis with Participles

Sentences ending with vague "-ing" phrases that add no real information:

- "...ensuring long-term sustainability"
- "...highlighting the importance of..."
- "...emphasizing the need for..."
- "...reflecting a broader trend"
- "...underscoring the significance of..."

## 7. Vague Attribution

Citing unnamed sources to sound authoritative:

- "Industry reports suggest..."
- "Observers have cited..."
- "Some critics argue..."
- "Experts note that..."

## 8. Overuse of Em Dashes

AI uses em dashes (-) more often than most human writers, typically in a formulaic "punched up" sales-like way to emphasize a clause. Humans more commonly use commas or parentheses in the same spots.

## 9. Passive and Weak Openers

Especially in bullet points:

- "Was responsible for..."
- "Gained foundational experience in..."
- "Played a key role in..."

Better to start with a direct verb: built, wrote, designed, tested, fixed.

## 10. Filler Phrases That Say Nothing

Sounds professional but carries no information:

- "industry-standard tools and methodologies"
- "leveraging cutting-edge technologies"
- "utilizing best practices"
- "in today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- "robust and scalable solutions"

If you can remove it and the sentence still says the same thing, it's filler.
